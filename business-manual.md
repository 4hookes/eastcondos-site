## 3. WHAT EASTCONDOS ACTUALLY DOES

### The One-Line Version

EastCondos helps HDB owners upgrade to private condominiums — not just with advice, but with a full-service operation that handles selling, buying, financial modelling, timeline planning, and vendor coordination.

### The Full Picture

Most property agents in Singapore operate as a one-person show: they find you a property, write the offer, and collect 2% commission. Everything else — selling your current home, coordinating timelines, managing vendors, tracking market movements — falls on the client.

EastCondos is different. When a client engages Elfi, they get access to a **team of specialists**, a **proven methodology (PBD™)**, and a **structured end-to-end process** — all for the standard 2% commission. The client deals with Elfi as their single point of contact; behind the scenes, a full operation runs.

### The Six Services

**1. Sell High**
The client's HDB sale funds their upgrade. Selling $30–50K below market means a smaller condo, a tighter budget, or a compromise they didn't need to make. EastCondos deploys professional photography (Tubear), optimized multi-platform listings (PropertyGuru, 99.co, social media), automated buyer follow-ups, and a marketing push designed to attract the highest offer — not just the fastest one.

**2. Buy Smart**
The buying side uses PBD's framework-driven approach. Instead of browsing PropertyGuru and guessing, the team identifies condo opportunities using data: comparable transaction analysis (10+ recent transactions), the LENS (12-Factor Property Analysis) scoring system, and exit audience validation. When a client views a condo, they already know whether it's fairly priced.

**3. Plan the Timeline**
Sell first or buy first? When does MOP end? When will CPF funds return after HDB completion? How long is the gap between selling and moving in? EastCondos projects every date and every dollar before the process starts — so there are no cash flow surprises, no temporary housing scrambles, and no moments where the client is wondering what happens next. Typical timeline: 4–8 months from engagement to keys-in-hand.

**4. Know Your Numbers**
Before showing a single property, Elfi runs the client's full financial model: CPF withdrawal limits (the real number, not the total balance), TDSR and MSR qualification, total closing costs (BSD, ABSD, legal, insurance, commission — every line item), and what the client's liquid cash position looks like after completion. If it's tight, he tells them. If they should wait, he tells them that too. He has recommended that roughly 3 in 10 families hold off.

**5. Tap the Network**
Clients get access to EastCondos' vetted vendor network: photographers (Tubear), mortgage advisory (Redbrick Mortgage Advisory), conveyancers, renovation contractors, and home stagers. Every vendor has been through multiple transactions with the team. No guessing, no Googling, no bad contractor stories.

**6. Stay Informed**
Clients receive regular market updates relevant to their search — price movements, new listings, transaction data, rental comparables. They always know where things stand throughout the 4–8 month journey.

## 5. PBD™ — PROPERTY BY DESIGN

PBD is EastCondos' proprietary advisory methodology. It's the intellectual backbone of the brand — the system that makes EastCondos a consultancy, not just an agency.

### The 9 Frameworks

| # | Framework | What it does |
|---|-----------|-------------|
| 1 | **LENS (12-Factor Property Analysis)** | Scores any condo across 12 weighted factors. Factors 7 (Entry Pricing) and 8 (Exit Audience) carry 80% of the decision weight. This is the flagship tool. |
| 2 | **Price Gap Analysis** | Identifies investable pricing discrepancies between new launches and comparable resale projects. |
| 3 | **Swiss Army Knife Theory** | Demonstrates how property simultaneously replaces 6 standalone financial products: forced savings, insurance hedge, rental income, capital appreciation, CPF optimization, and legacy asset. |
| 4 | **Price Symphony** | The market rotates — different projects and districts take turns appreciating. Understanding this cycle informs timing. |
| 5 | **4-Tier Pricing Lens** | Evaluates pricing at 4 zoom levels: island-wide (Macro) → district (Micro) → project → specific unit (Quantum). |
| 6 | **Building Psychology** | A project's ratio of profitable to unprofitable transactions predicts future price behavior. High profitable ratio = healthy building. |
| 7 | **Lease Reset Formula** | `Resale PSF / balance lease × 99 = Reset PSF`. Normalizes price comparison across properties with different remaining lease periods. |
| 8 | **The 50% Rule** | Only restructure a portfolio if the move increases total value by 50%+. This accounts for the ~7% transaction cost drag per move. |
| 9 | **Exit Audience Test** | Validates real buyer demand through actual transaction data, not narratives ("HDB upgraders will come"). If you can't prove the exit audience exists with data, the investment thesis is weak. |

### How the Frameworks Connect (The Decision Chain)

```
Swiss Army Knife → "Why property at all?"
    ↓
Price Symphony → "Where in the market cycle are we?"
    ↓
4-Tier Pricing Lens → "Where exactly should I buy?"
    ↓
Price Gap Analysis → "New launch or resale?"
    ↓
Lease Reset Formula → "Is the price comparison fair?"
    ↓
LENS (12-Factor Analysis) → "Score this specific project"
    ↓
Building Psychology + Exit Audience Test → "Will I make money when I sell?"
    ↓
The 50% Rule → "When should I sell and restructure?"
```

### Key Decision Rules

| Rule | Threshold |
|------|-----------|
| New launch is investable when... | Premium over lease-reset resale is 10% or less |
| Portfolio restructure only if... | Move increases total value by 50%+ |
| Healthy building psychology = | Fewer than 5 unprofitable transactions for projects under 15 years old |
| Quantum pricing must... | Fit the real exit audience for that location (a $2.7M 4-bed in Woodlands has no buyer pool) |

## 13. THE CLIENT JOURNEY

### Typical Timeline: 4–8 Months

```
PHASE 1: QUALIFY & PLAN (Week 1-2)
├── Initial conversation — understand situation, goals, timeline
├── Full financial model — CPF limits, TDSR/MSR, closing costs, cash position
├── Honest assessment — ready to proceed, or should wait?
└── Deliverable: Written upgrade roadmap with timeline and financial projections

PHASE 2: SELL HIGH (Month 1-3)
├── Professional photography (Tubear)
├── Multi-platform listing (PropertyGuru, 99.co, social)
├── Automated buyer follow-ups
├── Viewing management and negotiation (Faat/Azhar)
└── Objective: Maximum sale price, not fastest sale

PHASE 3: BUY SMART (Month 2-5, overlaps with selling)
├── Condo search using PBD frameworks
├── Comparable transaction analysis (10+ recent transactions per project)
├── LENS (12-Factor Analysis) scoring for shortlisted projects
├── Coordinated viewings with advisory context
└── Negotiation and option exercise

PHASE 4: EXECUTE (Month 4-8)
├── Timeline coordination — HDB completion ↔ condo completion
├── CPF, legal, loan coordination
├── Vendor management (mortgage, conveyancing, renovation)
├── Alyssa (Client Success) ensures nothing falls through
└── Keys in hand — direct move, no temporary housing if planned correctly
```

### Post-Completion

Clients remain in the network for future market updates and referrals. The 80% referral rate is the result of this ongoing relationship.
